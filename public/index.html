Most Influential Death Metal Bands

1. Death

Death, formed in 1983 by Chuck Schuldiner, is often considered the pioneer of death metal. Their albums like "Scream Bloody Gore" and "Leprosy" set the standards for the genre.

2. Morbid Angel

Morbid Angel, founded in 1983, is known for their influential albums like "Altars of Madness" and "Blessed Are the Sick." Their unique style contributed significantly to the genre.

3. Cannibal Corpse

Cannibal Corpse, formed in 1988, is famous for their brutal and extreme lyrics. Albums like "Tomb of the Mutilated" and "The Bleeding" solidified their place in death metal history.

4. Obituary

Obituary emerged in 1984 and released iconic albums such as "Cause of Death" and "The End Complete." They are known for their distinctive growling vocals and heavy riffs.

5. Entombed

Entombed, formed in 1987, combined death metal with elements of punk and rock. Their debut album "Left Hand Path" is a classic in the genre.

6. Morbid Visions

Morbid Visions, hailing from Brazil, was founded in 1984. Their early work, especially the album "Morbid Visions," had a profound impact on death metal.
